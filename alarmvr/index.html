AlarmVR: A Virtual ICU to Investigate Alarm Fatigue
This project employs VR and immersive audio to explore the underlying auditory causes of one of the top patient safety hazards in clinical environments, namely alarm fatigue, which is a form of sensory overload that causes healthcare professionals to become desensitized towards alarm events due to extended exposure. Recent trends in clinical alarm research promote a participatory approach to alarm design, where stakeholders, including healthcare providers, patients, and visitors, are involved in the design process. Accordingly, AlarmVR aims to bring these stakeholders into an immersive context where they can reflect upon current and future alarm sounds in clinical environments. In this video, we see the immersive documentation of an ICU's at the Clinical Simulation Center. This documentation guided our virtualizing of an ICU in VR. You can drag the view to explore the ICU panoramically.

Forum Acousticum Paper
Here, we see an artistic application of AlarmVR, where it is used for creating an interactive rendition of Terry Riley's seminal work "In C." Titled In(tensive) C(are), this project allows the performer to use medical alarms as the instruments that play the sequences of Riley's piece. The performer can control the dynamic and timbral qualities of the alarms to alter the instrumentation. They can also change sequences, randomize the sound designs, and control the tempo and volume of the piece. The alarms are rendered in binaural audio, resulting in an immersive interpretation of "In C" in an interactive virtual ICU.

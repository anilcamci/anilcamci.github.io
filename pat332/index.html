PAT 332: Sound Recording & Production II
In this video, I cover some of the hands-on activities that my students and I engage in PAT 332 Sound Recording & Production II. In the first segment, the students are exploring various Ambisonic and binaural microphones. We start by creating spatial audio content with students playing different instruments. We then learn how to work with Ambisonic signals in a digital audio workstation in a production context. Throughout the semester, they also gain hands-on experience with more traditional recording practices, like how to microphone a piano or drums. In the next segment, we see a 6-minute extract from one of our class sessions, where we apply our experience with recording drums to the design of electronic drum sounds using a software modular synthesizer. In a following assignment, students work in groups to apply these principles to a project that focuses on electronic music production.

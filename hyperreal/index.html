Hyperreal Instruments: Musical Systems that Bridge VR and Fabrication
Hyperreal Instruments project bridges VR and digital fabrication in the design of new virtual instruments that can defy our sense of audiovisual reality while satisfying our haptic expectations. By coupling the virtual and the fabricated manifestations of a digital design using the spatial tracking technologies integrated into modern VR systems, this project investigates the sensory mappings across the visual, auditory, and haptic modalities. In this video, I demonstrate a Hyperreal Instrument that incorporates the repelling force between neodymium magnets. This way, a VR instrument, which is inherently devoid of tactility, is imbued with haptic feedback, an essential affordance of most musical instruments
